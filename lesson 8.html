What is the Present Perfect? The Present Perfect tense is used to describe actions that have been completed at this moment or have a connection to the present. It’s useful for talking about past experiences, results, and actions that continue into the present.

2. Present Perfect Formula

Structure: Subject + have/has + past participle (V3)

Examples:

  • I have visited Paris.
  • She has finished her homework.

Note: Use have with I, you, we, they and has with he, she, it.

3. When to Use the Present Perfect

  • To describe experiences
    Example: I have been to New York.
  • For actions that started in the past and continue to the present
    Example: We have lived here for five years.
  • For actions with results that are important now
    Example: She has lost her keys (and can’t get into the house).

4. Key Words and Time Markers

Keywords: just, already, yet, ever, never

Examples:

  • I have just finished my work.
  • Have you ever visited Japan?

Family and Friends: Describing Relationships

Building Long-Lasting Relationships

I have known my best friend, Emily, for over ten years. We met in school, and since then, our friendship has grown stronger. We have shared so many wonderful moments together, from vacations to family gatherings, and our bond has only become deeper with time. We’ve always supported each other, whether it was during tough times or moments of celebration.

Changes in Relationships

Over the years, some of my family relationships have changed. My brother, for example, has become much more independent since he moved to university. Before that, he often needed help from our parents, but now he manages everything on his own. We have had a good relationship, but recently, we have started spending more time together, which has brought us closer.

Experiences with Family and Friends

I have had many experiences with my friends that I will never forget. Last summer, we traveled to Italy together. It was an unforgettable trip, and I have made memories that will last forever. My family and I have visited many countries, and we’ve enjoyed exploring new places and learning about different cultures.

I have also been lucky enough to meet some wonderful people through work. Some of my colleagues have become good friends. We have had lunch together every week since I started my job, and that’s helped us get to know each other better.
