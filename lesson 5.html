Phrasal Verbs in English

Phrasal Verbs in English

Definition

Phrasal verbs are verbs combined with one or more particles that create a different meaning.

Structure

Form: Verb + Particle(s)

  • Example: give up, look after, run into

Types of Phrasal Verbs

1. Transitive

Require a direct object.

Example: She gave up smoking.

2. Intransitive

Do not take a direct object.

Example: He woke up late.

3. Separable

The object can come between the verb and the particle.

Example: She turned down the offer. / She turned it down.

4. Inseparable

The object must follow the particle.

Example: He ran into an old friend.

Common Phrasal Verbs

  • Bring up: to mention a topic
  • Get along: to have a good relationship
  • Carry on: to continue
  • Put off: to postpone
  • Take over: to assume control

Practice and Usage

Reading, listening, and using phrasal verbs in conversation can enhance fluency.

Conclusion

Mastering phrasal verbs is essential for fluency and comprehension in English.

The Basics of English: Greetings and Introductions

When you arrive at the airport, the first thing to do is check in for your flight. You can use a self-service kiosk or go to the airline counter. After checking in, you will drop off your luggage and receive a boarding pass. Next, you must go through security, where you show your ID and boarding pass. After passing through the scanner, you can collect your belongings and find your gate. Once at the gate, listen for any announcements about your flight. After arriving at your destination, head to the hotel where you have a reservation. Check in at the front desk, providing your name and booking details. If everything is correct, you’ll receive your room key. Finally, take a moment to unpack and explore the hotel’s amenities.

  5. What do you do after arriving at your destination?

Writing

Task 1: Describe Your Arrival at the Airport

Instructions: Write a short paragraph (10 sentences) describing your arrival at the airport and the steps you take to check in and go through security. Use at least five phrasal verbs from the list below.

Phrasal Verbs: check in, drop off, go through, look for, pick up

Example Structure:

  1. Begin with your arrival at the airport.
  2. Describe the check-in process.
  3. Mention what you do with your luggage.
  4. Talk about going through security.
  5. Conclude with what you do after security.

Task 2: Your Hotel Experience

Instructions: Write about your experience checking into a hotel (10 sentences) using at least five phrasal verbs from the list below.

Phrasal Verbs: check in, fill out, ask for, settle in, run into

Example Structure:

  1. Start with your arrival at the hotel.
  2. Describe how you check in.
  3. Mention any special requests you make.
  4. Talk about unpacking and settling into your room.
  5. Include any interactions with hotel staff or other guests.

Task 3: Travel Plans and Packing

Instructions: Write about your travel plans and the packing process (10 sentences), using at least five phrasal verbs from the list below.

Phrasal Verbs: look forward to, pack up, take off, plan out, get ready

Example Structure:

  1. Begin with your excitement for the trip.
  2. Describe how you plan your packing.
  3. Mention the items you need to pack.
  4. Talk about checking your travel documents.
  5. Conclude with your feelings as you prepare for takeoff.

Task 1: Role-Play: The Airport Experience

Instructions: Pair up with a partner. One person will act as a traveler, and the other will be an airport staff member. Create a dialogue based on the following scenario. Speak for about 3-5 minutes.

Scenario:

  1. The traveler asks about checking in using a kiosk.
  2. The staff member explains how to drop off luggage.
  3. The traveler inquires about security procedures.
  4. The staff member gives directions to the gate.
  5. The traveler expresses excitement about their destination.

Task 2: Travel Storytelling

Instructions: Prepare a 2-3 minute story about a memorable trip you took, focusing on navigating airports and hotels. Incorporate creative details and feelings to engage your listeners. Use these prompts:

  1. Describe arriving at the airport and your first impressions.
  2. Mention any challenges you faced during check-in or security.
  3. Talk about your experience at the hotel—did anything unexpected happen?
  4. Highlight a memorable interaction with hotel staff or other travelers.
  5. Conclude with what you learned from the experience.

Task 3: Create a Travel Guide

Instructions: Imagine you are a travel guide. In a 3-5 minute presentation, give tips on how to navigate airports and hotels effectively. Use the following points as a guide:

  1. Share tips for preparing before heading to the airport.
  2. Discuss how to make the check-in process smoother.
  3. Explain what to expect at security and how to stay calm.
  4. Provide advice for checking into a hotel and what to look for.
  5. Include some fun anecdotes or advice about traveling in general.
