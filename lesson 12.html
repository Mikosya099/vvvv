Future Simple Tense - Grammar Rule

The Future Simple tense is used to talk about actions or events that will happen in the future.

Structure:

  • Positive sentences: Subject + will + base verb
  • Negative sentences: Subject + will not (won't) + base verb
  • Questions: Will + subject + base verb?

Examples:

  • Positive: I will study tomorrow. (The action will happen in the future.)
  • Negative: She will not (won't) go to the party. (She is not going to the party in the future.)
  • Question: Will you travel next year? (Asking about a future action.)

Usage:

We use the Future Simple tense for:

  • Decisions made at the moment of speaking: I'll have the pizza.
  • Promises and offers: I will help you with your homework.
  • Predictions about the future: It will rain tomorrow.
  • Future facts: The sun will rise tomorrow.

Reading

Future Plans: Dreams and Goals

Hi! My name is Alex, and I want to tell you about my future dreams and goals.

In the next few years, I will graduate from university with a degree in business. After that, I will work hard to build my career. My dream job is to become a manager at a big company. I will lead a team and will help my colleagues grow. One day, I will start my own business. It will be a small coffee shop with a cozy atmosphere, where people will relax and enjoy their time.

But life isn’t just about work. I also dream about my future family. I will marry someone who shares my values, and we will have two children. We will live in a house with a beautiful garden. On weekends, we will go on family trips to the mountains or the beach.

I also have personal goals. I will learn to play the guitar and will write my own songs. I will travel to different countries, like Spain and Australia. In Spain, I will visit famous landmarks, and in Australia, I will explore nature and see kangaroos!

No matter what happens, I will try my best to achieve my dreams and stay happy. What about you? What are your dreams and goals?
